I am Ke Wang, but feel free to call me Lucas. I graduated from Cornell University with dual B.A. degrees in Biological Sciences and Computer Science, concentrating in neuroscience and machine learning. My academic path was profoundly inspired by Frank Rosenblatt, a pioneering Cornell faculty member who founded modern deep learning. During my early undergraduate years, I was surprised to discover that Rosenblatt was trained purely as a neuroscientist with no formal computer science or mathematics background. Drawing inspiration solely from neurophysiology, he developed the perceptronâ€”the foundational algorithm that gave rise to today's deep learning systems that recognize our voices and understand our language.

My interest in biology started broad and comes to converge onto neuroscience in college. While working at the Bass Lab during semesters, I attended the CIBR-McGovern Summer Neuroscience institute in freshman summer, interned at my Cornell undergraduate lab in my sophomore summer, and did summer research at the Neuroscholar Summer Research Program at UCLA with scholarship in my junior year summer. Following graduation, I joined the Lee Laboratory at Harvard Medical School, where I investigate how working memory is encoded in cortical neural population dynamics using computational approaches. My research experience spans systems neuroscience, connectomics, behavioral analysis, and computational modeling across multiple model systems.

Research Areas

Rodent Spatial Navigation

With physical and VR mazes setups, navigational paradigms give us a good window for various aspects of brain function.

Neural Basis of Working Memory

How sensory information no longer present is stored and retrieved for task on long behavioral time scales.

Neural Basis of Recall & Imagination

How circuits like the hippocampus and medial prefrontal cortex represent spatial location that is distant from the animal's current location.

Reinforcement-Learning Agents

How reinforcement agents address the same task that our animal faces, and what interesting property emerges from our agent's parameters.

Cross Area Communication

How information is communicated between brain areas at different stages of an experiment, and the distinctive computational role they play respectively.

Connectomics & Axon Tracing

Investigating the connection pattern between differnt regions with electron miscroscope technology and virus-based tracing constructs.

Neural Dynamics Analysis Methods

Novel analysis methods to reduce high-dimensional neural dynamics to interpretable low-dimensional representations.

Electrophysiology & Calcium Imaging

Using electrophysiology and calcium imaging to read out the high-dimensional neural dynamics of cortical and subcortical regions.
