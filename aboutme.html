About Me

I am Ke Wang, but feel free to call me Lucas. I graduated from Cornell with B.A. in Biological Science and in Computer Science, where I concentrated in the discipline of Neuroscience and Machine Learning. My choice of academic path is very inspitred by Frank Rosenblatt, the founder of modern deep learning. I learned about this Cornell facuty in my early undergraduate career, where I was surprised to find out that he was purely a neuroscience faculty with no computer science or mathematics background. Inspired by neurophysiology alone, he created the ancestral algorithm (perceptron) of deep learning that now recognizes our voices and speaks our language.

My interest in biology started broad and comes to converge onto neuroscience in college. While working at the Bass Lab during semesters, I attended the CIBR-McGovern Summer Neuroscience institute in freshman summer, interned at my Cornell undergraduate lab in my sophomore summer, and did summer research at the Neuroscholar Summer Research Program at UCLA with scholarship in my junior year summer. After graduation, I've been working at Lee Lab at Harvard Medical School, working on understanding how working memory is represented in cortical neural dynamics with broad machine learning methods. I've had experience working in both system neuroscience, connectomics, behavior and many other topics.

Research Areas

Rodent Spatial Navigation

With physical and VR mazes setups, navigational paradigms give us a good window for various aspects of brain function.

Neural Basis of Working Memory

How sensory information no longer present is stored and retrieved for task on long behavioral time scales.

Neural Basis of Recall & Imagination

How circuits like the hippocampus and medial prefrontal cortex represent spatial location that is distant from the animal's current location.

Reinforcement-Learning Agents

How reinforcement agents address the same task that our animal faces, and what interesting property emerges from our agent's parameters.

Cross Area Communication

How information is communicated between brain areas at different stages of an experiment, and the distinctive computational role they play respectively.

Connectomics & Axon Tracing

Investigating the connection pattern between differnt regions with electron miscroscope technology and virus-based tracing constructs.

Neural Dynamics Analysis Methods

Novel analysis methods to reduce high-dimensional neural dynamics to interpretable low-dimensional representations.

Electrophysiology & Calcium Imaging

Using electrophysiology and calcium imaging to read out the high-dimensional neural dynamics of cortical and subcortical regions.

Research Skill Set

Machine Learning Based Statistical Analysis

Recording from vast numbers of neurons in awake animals has become remarkably accessible. With this abundance of data, systems neuroscientists now face the challenge of showing that these complex, high-dimensional neural patterns can be distilled into low-dimensional, interpretable forms. Benefiting from my familirity with common ML architectures, methods, and frameworks, I am doing NLP in neuroscience, intrepreting the unique natural language between happening in rodent circuits.

Reinforcement Learning Agents Modeling

The same behavioral paradigm might require similar characteristics from agents performing its task, no matter that agent is an animal or a RL agent. Using transformer-based RL agents, I model our delayed nonmatch-to-sample task as a POMDP (partially-observable markovian decision process), and look at what interesting property emerges in my agent.
